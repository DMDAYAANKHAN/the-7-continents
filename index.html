Asia


  1. Asia is Earth's largest and most populous continent, located primarily in the Eastern and Northern Hemispheres.

  2. It shares the continental landmass of Eurasia with the continent of Europe and the continental landmass of Afro-Eurasia with both Europe and Africa.

  3. Asia covers an area of 44,579,000 square kilometres (17,212,000 sq mi), about 30% of Earth's total land area and 8.7% of the Earth's total surface area.

  4. In general terms, Asia is bounded on the east by the Pacific Ocean, on the south by the Indian Ocean, and on the north by the Arctic Ocean.

  5. The boundary between Asia and Africa is the Red Sea, the Gulf of Suez, and the Suez Canal.

Africa


  1. Africa is the world's second-largest and second-most populous continent, after Asia in both cases

  2. At about 30.3 million km including adjacent islands, it covers 6% of Earth's total surface area and 20% of its land area.

  3. With 1.3 billion people as of 2018, it accounts for about 16% of the world's human population.

  4. Early human civilizations, such as Ancient Egypt and Carthage emerged in North Africa.

  5. Africa straddles the equator and the prime meridian making it the only continent in the world to be situated in all four cardinal hemispheres.

North America


  1. North America is a continent in the Northern Hemisphere and almost entirely within the Western Hemisphere.

  2. North America covers an area of about 24,709,000 square kilometers (9,540,000 square miles), about 16.5% of the Earth's land area and about 4.8% of its total surface.

  3. North America was reached by its first human populations during the last glacial period, via crossing the Bering land bridge approximately 40,000 to 17,000 years ago.

  4. It can also be described as the northern subcontinent of a single continent, America.

  5. Because it is on the North American Tectonic Plate, Greenland is included as part of North America geographically.

South America


  1. South America is a continent entirely in the Western Hemisphere and mostly in the Southern Hemisphere, with a relatively small portion in the Northern Hemisphere.

  2. South America is bordered on the west by the Pacific Ocean and on the north and east by the Atlantic Ocean, North America and the Caribbean Sea lie to the northwest.

  3. South America has an area of 17,840,000 square kilometers (6,890,000 sq mi).

  4. Its population as of 2018 has been estimated at more than 423 million.

  5. Most of the population lives near the continent's western or eastern coasts while the interior and the far south are sparsely populated.

Antarctica


  1. Antarctica is Earth's southernmost continent.

  2. At 14,200,000 square kilometres (5,500,000 square miles), it is the fifth-largest continent and nearly twice the size of Australia.

  3. Antarctica, on average, is the coldest, driest, and windiest continent, and has the highest average elevation of all the continents.

  4. Antarctica is governed by parties to the Antarctic Treaty System.

  5. It supports scientific research and protects the continent's ecology.

Europe


  1. Europe is a landmass located entirely in the Northern Hemisphere and mostly in the Eastern Hemisphere.

  2. Europe covers about 10,180,000 km2 (3,930,000 sq mi), or 2% of the Earth's surface (6.8% of land area), making it the second smallest continent

  3. European culture is the root of Western civilization, which traces its lineage back to ancient Greece and ancient Rome.

  4. In 1949, the Council of Europe was founded with the idea of unifying Europe to achieve common goals and prevent future wars.

  5. The EU originated in Western Europe but has been expanding eastward since the fall of the Soviet Union in 1991.

Australia


  1. Australia, officially the Commonwealth of Australia, is a sovereign country comprising the mainland of the Australian continent, the island of Tasmania, and numerous smaller islands.

  2. It is the largest country by area in Oceania and the world's sixth-largest country.

  3. Australia's population of nearly 26 million,[7] in an area of 7,617,930 square kilometres (2,941,300 sq mi)

  4. Canberra is the nation's capital, while the largest city is Sydney, and other major metropolitan areas include Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, and Adelaide.

  5. Australia is a highly developed country, with the world's twelfth-largest economy.
